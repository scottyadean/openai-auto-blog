5 Of the Best Small Towns To Vist in New England



New England is full of small towns, each with its own unique charm and local attractions. Whether you're looking for a weekend getaway or a longer vacation, these five towns are some of the best that New England has to offer.

1. Mystic, Connecticut: Mystic is a small town located in southeastern Connecticut. It is renowned for its beautiful harbor, historic lighthouses, and maritime museums. It's also home to the Mystic Aquarium, Mystic Seaport, and the Mystic River.

2. Portsmouth, New Hampshire: Portsmouth is a picturesque small town located on the coast of New Hampshire. This town is full of colonial charm, with historic buildings, cobblestone streets, and a waterfront area. It also has plenty of shopping, dining, and cultural attractions, as well as a vibrant nightlife.

3. Bar Harbor, Maine: Bar Harbor is a small town located on the east coast of Maine. This town is known for its natural beauty, with breathtaking views of the coast, mountains, and islands. It's also home to Acadia National Park, where visitors can explore the trails, beaches, and forests.

4. Woodstock, Vermont: Woodstock is a quintessential New England village located in the Green Mountains of Vermont. This town is full of colonial charm, with cobblestone streets, historic buildings, and plenty of shops and restaurants. It's also home to the Billings Farm and Museum, which offers a look at rural life in 19th century Vermont.

5. Lenox, Massachusetts: Lenox is a charming small town located in the Berkshires of western Massachusetts. This town is known for its beautiful natural surroundings, with lush forests, tranquil lakes, and rolling hills. It's also home to many cultural attractions, such as Tanglewood and the Berkshire Theater Festival.
